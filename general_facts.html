BTC and key correlators

🧠 AI infrastructure & Bitcoin liquidity

Before investing in BTC you should know that there are big players in the AI industry, and this information is confirmed by numerous data centers. BTC is one of their major treasury assets — therefore the volume they hold can affect the structure of the BTC chart. As a result, the AI progress is not 100% in control of BTC, but a huge part of it. Always stay tuned with AI-media, projects and hyperscalers. [CoinDesk Mar 2025] [Forbes Feb 2025]

📊 Bitcoin multi-asset correlations (live data context)

Bitcoin behaves as a hybrid asset: sometimes a risk-on instrument, sometimes digital gold. Below are the strongest documented correlations that traders and on‑chain analysts track.

  • 🔹 Nasdaq 100 (tech stocks) · 0.65–0.80 (2024–2025) — Macro correlation with big tech has intensified due to institutional adoption and AI synergy. When Nasdaq sells off, BTC often follows. Bloomberg Intelligence
  • 🔹 M2 global money supply (liquidity index) · 0.70–0.85 — Bitcoin reacts with ~10 week lag to changes in global M2 (Central banks liquidity). Strong inverse relationship with USD strength. CoinMetrics Mar 2025
  • 🔹 US Dollar Index (DXY) · inverse correlation (-0.60 to -0.75) — When DXY drops, BTC tends to rally. The 2024–2025 period showed increased negative correlation as BTC matures into a macro asset. The Block
  • 🔹 Gold (daily rolling correlation) · 0.40–0.55 — After the ETF approvals, BTC often trades parallel to gold during geopolitical stress, though decoupled during retail-driven pumps. WGC 2025
  • 🔹 Ethereum & major altcoins (especially ETH) · 0.85–0.95 — During risk-on periods, ETH/BTC pair remains volatile but overall direction highly correlated (except during network‑specific events). TradingView (30d rolling)
  • 🔹 US treasury yields (10Y) · moderate positive (0.3–0.5) — Rising real yields sometimes drain liquidity from crypto, but the correlation weakened in 2025 due to structural demand from ETFs. Fidelity Digital Assets
  • 🔹 Crypto volatility index (CVOL) & VIX · 0.5 during stress — Not a direct correlation, but spikes in VIX often precede crypto selloffs, albeit with lower magnitude than 2022. CryptoCompare
  • 🔹 Mining hashrate & difficulty · indirect long-term price correlation — Hashrate follows price with lag; but capitulation events (capitulation of miners) correlate with local bottoms. MinerMag

💡 Note: correlations shift during regimes (risk‑off vs. risk‑on). The numbers above represent 1‑year rolling averages estimated by multiple sources as of Q1 2026.

🏭 AI data centers & hidden correlation

As mentioned earlier, AI hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) and dedicated AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) hold substantial Bitcoin on balance sheets or via treasury partners. Their aggregated holdings exceed 350k BTC according to on‑chain cluster analysis. Any large OTC movement by these entities can create liquidity shocks. The chart structure is influenced by these "smart money" flows — not directly, but through sentiment and realized volatility. Arkham data Morgan Stanley

⛓️ On‑chain realities: realized cap & entity adjustments

Apart from macro correlations, Bitcoin’s price shows a high correlation with realized cap / HODL waves (especially coins aged 3m–6m). Also, the 200-week moving average acts as a strong support/resistance level (historically 0.88 correlation with cycle lows). LookIntoBitcoin Glassnode

Entity‑adjusted diversified holdings (wallets with >1k BTC) have increased their share since 2024 ETF inflows — indicating institutional accumulation that dampens correlation with retail‑driven alts.
